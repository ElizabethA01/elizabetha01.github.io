01 — About

The switch

I spent over five years as a Chartered Mechanical Engineer, including 1.5 years as an Assistant Project Manager at a global engineering and infrastructure consultancy. Frustrated by how much of that role relied on manual, repetitive processes, I taught myself Python to automate my own workload — chasing invoices, parsing contractor emails, wrangling spreadsheets. That first project is still on GitHub — the actual starting point, not just a line on a CV.

What started as a way to save time became a discovery: I loved solving problems through code more than I expected to. That realisation gave me the confidence to leave a career I'd trained years for, and switch into tech entirely — around three and a half years ago.

Both roles left their mark. The structured, safety-conscious rigor of mechanical engineering shows up in how I approach system design and code review; the stakeholder management and risk communication I built as a Project Manager now shapes how I lead cross-team migrations and mentor engineers finding their way into the industry.

04 — Community

WomenCoders & mentoring

I've led on the WomenCoders Leadership Team at TripAdvisor since June 2024 — a global initiative supporting women in engineering across the US and UK. I've contributed to improvements in TripAdvisor's interview process, ensuring it works for candidates from non-traditional backgrounds.

Beyond TripAdvisor, I mentor individuals switching into tech from non-traditional backgrounds. About three to four of the people I've mentored have since made the switch themselves, moving into roles in cybersecurity and software engineering.
